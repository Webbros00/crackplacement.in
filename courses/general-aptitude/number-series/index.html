Number Series




The basic five types of number series are as mentioned and defined below:

Ration series: - These series consist of a sequence of numbers, fractions and integers, in which each term may or may not be different from the other term, with a set mathematical concept and operation (/, *, -, +) or formula can be applied to calculate the missing or the upcoming term.

Geometric series: - These series consists of a number sequence that are obtained by either (/, *, +, -) the previous number with a fixed common number, it is generally found either in an ascending or descending order.

Mixed series: - Consists of a set or series of numbers whose calculation might require either one or more than one mathematical concept.

Perfect square series: - Consists of a sequence of numbers that are perfect squares.

Perfect cube series: - Consists of a set order of numbers that are perfect cubes of the given term.


Following are some basic types number series (generalization basis):

Sum up Series (+): - adding a constant term to every previous term to get the next. For example: (14, 18, 22, 26 ….).

Difference Series (-): - subtracting a constant number from every previous term to get the next. For example: (26, 22, 18, 14 ……).

Step up Series (+/-): - a variable number is added or subtracted, here we are adding and subtracting 2n for n = 1, 2, 3 …… For example: 2, 6, 12, 20…. or 20, 12, 6, 2, ….

Square up (+/-): - here this series is in the square of n where n i = 0,1,2,3,4,5,6… i.e. 1,4,9,16, 25...

Square add up Series (+/-): - Adding n2 every time and incrementing value of n starting from 2 i.e. 2,6, 15, 31, 56, …… and Subtracting n2 every time and decrementing value of n i.e. 31, 30, 26, 11….

Cube Up Series: - Given series is in the cube of n (n3) where n i = 1, 2, 3, … i.e. 1, 8, 27, 64…. and so on

Cube add up Series: - Adding n3 every time with incrementing of n by 1 i.e. 1,9,36,100…

Prime up (+/-): - Series comprises of Prime numbers i.e. 1,3,5,7,11…

Prime Square up (+/-): - Series comprises of square of prime numbers i.e. 1,4,9,25,49,100,121...

Arithmetic Series: - Series comprises of Arithmetic Progression i.e. 4,8,12, …

Geometric Series: - Series comprises of Geometric Progression i.e. 1,3,9,27, …
